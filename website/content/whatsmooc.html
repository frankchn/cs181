What's a MOOC?

A massive open online course (MOOC) is a course aimed at large-scale audiences -- in the tens to hundreds of thousands -- delivered via the Internet. MOOCs typically do not charge tuition fees or offer academic credit.

Timeline

The current revolution in MOOCs started with three classes offered by Stanford Computer Science professors in Fall 2011.

Date Event
June 2011 Professor Sebastian Thrun of Stanford announces that he is going to offer his Artificial Intelligence class (CS221) for free on ai-class.com. Attracts over 100,000 people for the course.
August 2011 Professor Andrew Ng and Professor Jennifer Widom offers their Machine Learning and Introduction to Databases courses online as ML-class and DB-class.
Janaury 2012 Sebastian Thrun launches Udacity.
April 2012 Andrew Ng and Daphne Koller launches Coursera with 4 university partners -- Princeton, Stanford, the University of Pennsylvania and the University of Michigan.
May 2012 The edX Consortium launches with Harvard and MIT as founding partners.
September 2012 Coursera expands to 30 international partners.
May 2013 Udacity announces Online Masters program in Computer Science with Georgia Tech.
May 2013 Coursera announces partnership with 10 state systems to provide blended learning platforms and content.

Courses and Students

The big three MOOC providers, Coursera, Udacity and edX offers a combined 461 courses from over 100 partner universities. The breadth of courses offered has expanded greatly from the original three Computer Science courses offered, with courses ranging from Modern and Contemporary American Poetry to Probabilistic Graphical Models.

In the largest MOOC provider, Coursera, over 3.5 million students have registered for accounts and courses have been subscribed to over ten million times by these students. Other MOOC providers report similar numbers, with edX and Udacity both reporting over 1 million students registered each.

Credentialing

There have been experiments to provide verifiable and accurate credentials for these online classes. edX and Udacity have both used Pearson testing centers to provide final exams and verifiable certificates while Coursera launched Signature Track, a system designed for online credentials verification using keystroke biometrics technology. These experiments are still the in very early stages and will evolve over time, responding to student and instructor demands.
